Sentiment Analysis for Understanding Financial News Trends

Recently many people believe market sentiments could be used for prediction and speculation of the market behavior. News has positive sentiment when the economy is doing well and conveys negative sentiment when it's doing poorly. In this project, we built a visualization tool to study the relationship between news sentiment and shifts in economic performance in Canada. More specifically, we studied the correlation of news sentiment with respect to the actual movement of six major economic indicators, including GDP, mortgage rates, interest rates, housing prices, TSX, and employment rate.

View Project

BERT for Financial News Analysis

We applied variants of Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT) and text-based Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN’s) to perform sentiment analysis of financial news articles in order to provide valuable information for decision making in the stock market. BERT has been pre-trained on a large text corpus for a self-learning task. We use the dataset from Matheus Gomes de Sousa et. al which contains 582 manually labelled news articles as positive and negative. We applied a BERT-Base model on this dataset and obtain a F1-score of 0.82.
